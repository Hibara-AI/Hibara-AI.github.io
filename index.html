About



Hi there! I am a master student at Department of Computer Science, Rutgers University.

My research interest revolves around Machine Learning, Cognitive Science and NLP.
But ultimately, what I really care about is the computational philosophy of interaction between real world and the grand, intricate but captivating human mental world, if it could ever exist, together with human senses(visions and languages are being widely studied, but there still remains imagine, dream, psychology, etc.), and how life could be justly aestheticised based on it in both theology and atheology terms.
One's full awareness rings that this might never be achieved by human civilization known so far, but one would always welcome anyone to have a conversation on it with a cup of whiskey. Cheers!

Prior to coming to Rutgers, I was an undergraduate student at Tongji University, major in Electrical Information Engineering and Applied Mathematics.

For personal leisure, I enjoy practicing the piano(classical), reading about philosophy, history and Chinese ancient literature, doing sports and hiking.

You can contact me through A@B, where A=charles.deng and B=rutgers.edu.
